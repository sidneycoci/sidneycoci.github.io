Double Exposure

In this project I used Adobe Photoshop to create a "double-exposure" photo. I used an image of a waterfall in a forest and superimposed it with an image of myself. The inspiration for this project was the idea of the human connection to nature, drawing on the idea of a "Mother Earth" figure. In the photo, there are many points at which the viewer cannot tell where the nature photo and the photo of myself begin, making it appear that they are one.

Intuition Magazine

In this project I used Adobe Indesign to create my own Magazine cover. I chose to do a magazine pertaining to spirituality with a fun and introspective approach. I feel that expressions of spirituality have become very important in today's society and felt that a magazine geared towards a younger demographic would do well considering this uptake in spiritual themes. As well as this, wellness and connection are two things I really value, so it felt fitting that I would focus my project on these areas.

Exposure Therapy

In my time working at the XR Studio I have taken on the Exposure Therapy in VR project. This project is for a pyschological study being conducted to test if virtual reality is a viable option for treatment for phobias. I was brought on as a UX Designer to better design the dashboard which showcases the participant's biometrics. This project is currently in progress with final design pending soon, but the student developer and I were happily able to present the demo version at UM's Inagural Computing Day, where we won People's Choice Best Demo.

Intuition Spread

Further contributing to my idea for Intuition Magazine, I created a spread that would be showcased on the inside of the magazine. This article is titled "Crystalized" and it details the uptake in popularity that crystal jewelry has been seeing. I thought that due to the nature of the magazine overall, this was a very fitting article as it details how people are incorporating crystals into their daily life in order to bring certain spiritual aspects to their life. I designed this spread using Adobe Indesign, and worked with the cut-out images in Adobe Photoshop in order to create the final project.
